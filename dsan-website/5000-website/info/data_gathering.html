Data Gathering

Since my problem statement focuses upon the impact of fiscal policy on macroeconomic variables, the data naturally consists of numerical or quantitative data based on time. For this topic, the most reliable websites for data collection were either to physically search for the data or through the usage of APIs (Application Programming Interface).

Data Source

Since one of the questions mainly attempts to compare the impacts of fiscal policies between Bangladesh which is an underdeveloped economic and India which is a developing economy, India's data was retrieved from FRED API. The FRED® API is a web service that allows developers to create programmes and construct apps that retrieve statistical information about the economy from the FRED® and ALFRED® websites hosted by the Federal Reserve Bank of St. Louis' Economic Research Division using Python (FRED API, n.d).

On the other hand, the data for Bangladesh has been retrived from 'wbstats' API which is an R-based interface, that is used for searching and downloading data from World Bank (wbstats, n.d).

Data Description

The data has been collected from 2000-2023.
S.No Data Description
1 Gross Domestic Product It quantifies a nation's economic performance by summing the value of all goods and services produced.
2 Gross Government Debt It reflects a government's cumulative financial obligations, encompassing loans, bonds, and other indebtedness.
3 Consumer Price Index It is a key measure used to track changes in the average prices of goods and services.
4 Import Rates It refers to the tariffs or duties imposed on goods and services when they are brought into a country.
5 Inflation Rate It quantifies the percentage increase in general price levels within an economy over a specified period.
6 Gross National Income It calculates a nation's total income, including earnings from abroad and minus foreign entities' income earned locally.
6 Employment Population It represents the ratio of employed individuals to the total working-age population, indicating labor force participation./td>
7 Unemployment Rate It measures the percentage of people who are actively seeking employment but currently without a job in the labor force.
8 Infant Mortality Rate It quantifies the number of infant deaths within the first year per 1,000 live births.
9 Life Expectancy Rate It indicates the average number of years a person can expect to live, typically at birth, within a specific population.
10 Engaged Workers It represents the number of people engaged in work out of the overall population.
11 Private Final Consumption It represents the total expenditure by households and nonprofit institutions serving households (NPISH) on goods and services for their own use.
